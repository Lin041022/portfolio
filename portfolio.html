01

The Mariana Trench

Week 2 Haiku Collaborative Vocabulary

— Haiku

Mariana Trench,


Giant shapes swim silent


Deep sea mystery

Reflection

During the second week's activity, I wrote a haiku titled "The Mariana Trench". My intention was to capture the mystery of the ocean. Our entire class first brainstormed a shared vocabulary list about the ocean. I selected and combined 5 specific words from the list to form the final structure. This work is important because it taught me that creation does not have to start from scratch. Learning to draw on a collective resource pool reduces the anxiety of facing a blank page and makes getting started much easier.

02

Rhythmic Soundscape

Week 4 Audio / Sound Group Collaboration

Reflection

During the fourth week's activity, our group created a rhythmic audio piece. Starting with no great ideas, we chose to combine the sounds of pen strokes on a desk, water bottle tapping, and vocal beats to build an unexpected soundtrack. We gathered to experiment with various object sounds and adjusted our individual rhythms to align with the team's overall beat. This work is significant because it perfectly explains team creativity — not based on someone's genius idea, but a simple pen click that inspired another member to add the dull sound of a water bottle, transforming each isolated noise into a cohesive work.

Reflection

During the fifth week, our group collaborated to write a short play titled "Secrets on the Beach". We wanted to create dramatic tension, so we chose to use a sudden rainstorm as an external catalyst to break the character's emotional deadlock and force the narrative toward its climax. Every plot twist was the result of group members responding to each other's ideas. My initial suggestion of a "secret" inspired someone else to propose the "moving to London" premise, which combined with the "downpour" to force the story to its conclusion — demonstrating that creative force is distributed throughout the entire team.

Reflection

In week 7, our group collaborated on a short film titled "The Price of Cheating". Facing an initial creative block, I proposed the central concept — a student caught cheating during an exam. My teammates then expanded on this framework, adding fresh plot turns and specific camera angles to convey psychological tension. This work is important because it directly breaks the myth of the "lone genius" and illustrates collective creativity. My initial idea became a reality because the team collectively developed the visual imagery and narrative, proving that the film's success is entirely dependent on the whole team.

Silly Painting

Week 10 Collaborative Painting Chance & Constraint

Reflection

In week 10, our group chose to create a silly painting aimed at visually merging completely unrelated, random words. The process began by tossing paper balls covered in words describing anything in the classroom — an exchange that completely stripped us of individual control over the creative materials. We then formed small groups and worked together to create a painting incorporating everyone's words. Creating it required us to listen to each member's words and find unexpected visual connections, demonstrating that combining different elements generates richer imagination than working alone.

A Creativity Manifesto

Reflection

During the week 12 activity, I wrote a piece titled "A Creativity Manifesto". We exchanged ideas about creativity through a "snowball" activity and completed a questionnaire. Two questions resonated strongly: disagreeing with "It's okay to get stuck" and strongly disagreeing with "It doesn't matter if your work looks different from others'". My groupmate helped me answer these, resolving issues that had been troubling me — his answers helped me break through creative pressure, making me realise that overcoming it is not a solitary struggle, but a problem solved with peer support.
